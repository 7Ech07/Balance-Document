Force-A-Nature

The knockback on this weapon is, more often than not, a liability. The first shot blasts the target out of range of the second shot, forcing the user to have to fight the weapon in order to use it to its full potential.

  • Goal: Make the weapon more reliable at dealing damage (and generally more consistent)
  • Changes:
    • (+) Removed: If ammo is unused when reloading, it is lost
    • (+) Force jumping no longer temporarily stuns the user (disables their ability to strafe)
    • (+) Increased bullets per shot to +40%
    • (~) This weapon has a modified spread pattern, with the extra four bullets forming a smaller ring inside of the standard one (see image)
    • (~) Reduced knockback
      • Horizontal knockback reduced to a 200% multiplier (was 300%)
      • Fixed vertical knockback now scales inversely with distance (from 0 at 400 HU to the current value of 268 HU/s at point-blank)
    • (-) Damage penalty increased to 20%
    • (-) Knockback does not stun enemies, takes their existing momentum into account, and respects knockback resistances/immunities

Baby Face's Blaster

Players often complain that Boost is too easily lost. A couple of stray bullets or a bit of splash damage is enough to drop a Scout from 520 HU/s back down to 360 HU/s. The Boost drain on double jump compounds this by making it harder to dodge explosives.

Aside from this, I rather like the current design. It focuses on providing options to both Scout and the other team. It tries to control the power of the Boost by giving the other team the ability to slow the Scout down with damage, instead of with penalties to other parts of the gun. This means the Scout is still able to do normal Scout things instead of being locked out of jumping or being forced to chip people from range.

  • Goal: Refine the current design to be more user-friendly and consistent
  • Changes:
    • (+) Ratio of damage to Boost lost changed to 1:2 (was 1:4)
    • (+) Boost loss from double jumping changed to 50% (was 75%)
    • (+) User passively build Boost (at a rate of 2%/sec)
    • (+) Fall damage does not drain Boost
    • (-) Ratio of damage to boost gained changed to 3:2 (was 1:1)
