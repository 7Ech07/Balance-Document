Frontier Justice

The design of this weapon – trade some of your power normally for a boost when you would otherwise be vulnerable – is interesting, but the current implementation creates some issues. In addition to being another weapon that can snowball off kills, enemies are often unable to tell if destroying any given Sentry will grant the Engie Crits, how many Crits the Engie gets, and whether or not he still has them when the weapon is stowed. I also dislike how the loadout system allows players to take advantage of the weapon's strength situationally without needing to engage with its downside.

  • Goal: Make the item more predictable to fight against, and incentivise more active use of the shotgun
  • Changes:
    • (+) Kills by the Engineer that are assisted by their Sentry grant 2 stored Crits (instead of 1)
    • (-) Only Sentries built while the weapon is equipped will store Revenge Crits
      • Sentries built with this weapon equipped will have a capacitor and antenna (similar to the device on the gun) attached to them for the sake of telegraphing
      • If this Sentry dies while you have a different primary equipped, you still do not get Revenge Crits
    • (-) Revenge Crits have a 10 second timer on use after Sentry destruction, after which they will be reset to 0
    • (~) Destruction of a Sentry that would cause the Engineer to gain Revenge Crits causes him to play a voiceline

Rescue Ranger

The last nerf to this item was intended to reduce its overall power due to the sheer amount of utility it provides. The change succeeded at this, but the item is still extremely powerful in defensive scenarios because of how it significantly reduces risk to the Engineer. I think a more targeted change to address this problem would make the weapon more fair.

  • Goal: Make it more difficult to sustain buildings from safety
  • Changes:
    • (+) Base damage increased to 50 (was 40)
    • (+) Repairs no longer cost Metal
    • (-) Ramp-up reduced to 120% (max damage unchanged)
    • (-) Healing is applied over time at a rate of 20 health/second
      • This effect can stack
    • (-) Buildings targeted by the Alt-fire undergo a brief deconstruction animation back into a toolbox before being teleported to the Engineer
      • Buildings deconstruct four times as fast as they take to build
      • The Engineer cannot pick up any other buildings during the deconstruction animation, and the building will self-destruct if the Engineer dies during it

Wrangler

The current version of the Wrangler is extremely good at stalling pushes due to its ability to create a 600 health object that can waste the vast majority of an Über’s duration on its own. This ability also requires fairly little effort from the Engineer player.

Removing the shield entirely would fix this problem, but it would also make the item really weak, as Sentries were tuned around the Engineer’s repair rate and can be quickly destroyed when not receiving in-combat repairs. Something must be given in compensation for the shield.

Lifesteal on the Sentry still allows it to tank more damage, but it requires the Engineer to use the item for its intended purpose, and still leaves the Sentry vulnerable to the things it’s designed to be vulnerable to.

  • Goal: Reduce the stalling capabilities of this item
  • Changes:
    • (+) Added: The Sentry heals 30% of the damage dealt while Wrangled
    • (+) While Wrangled, Sentry rocket fire rate is properly increased by 50%
    • (+) Un-Wrangling a Sentry only disables it for 1 second
    • (-) Removed: 66% damage and repair reduction on the Sentry while active

Short Circuit

The previous rework of this item was intended to make it more skillful and more reliant on positioning and anticipation. It was a resounding failure. The energy ball takes significantly less timing and effort to use than the old electrical field attack, while being able to cover a significant area for a whole two seconds at a time.

The general concept of the Short Circuit is questionable, both in terms of how it tries to negate one of the pillars of the game’s combat, and in how it's largely restricted to fighting two classes. I’ve decided to de-emphasise projectile destruction and add other utilities.

  • Goal: New Design – Multi-purpose utility weapon that runs on Metal
    • (i) Fires a short-distance electrical arc
      • This attack fires a hitscan beam with 500 HU of range that deals damage on contact with the collision hull (making it a little more forgiving than normal hitscan)
      • 0.6 second cooldown
      • Deals 15 damage
    • (+) Hold primary fire to charge the weapon, increasing damage and Metal cost. Fully-charged shots destroy projectiles
      • Damage increases to 50
      • The projectile destruction uses a cylinder with more forgiveness than the hitscan trace (but requires more precision than previous iterations of the weapon)
      • 0.5 seconds to fully charge, another 0.5 seconds to overcharge (see below)
    • (-) Overcharging causes an explosion
      • The explosion occurs in front of the user (like detonating a Detonator flare point-blank) and deals 30 damage
    • (-) Uses Metal for ammo
    • (-) Per shot: 10–40 Metal

Gunslinger & Mini-Sentry

The Gunslinger is a somewhat polarising weapon. It's especially threatening to short-range classes who struggle to effectively kill the Mini-Sentries. However, the Minis are more of an annoyance than a threat to classes like Demo, and in my opinion the Gunslinger doesn't keep up with the Jag even for aggressive play.

  • Goal: Make the Mini-Sentry less annoying and more powerful
  • Changes:
    • (+) Mini-Sentry base construction speed increased to pre-Gun Mettle levels
    • (-) Mini-Sentry bullets do not deal knockback or inflict flinch
    • (-) Mini-Sentry turn speed reduced to that of a Level 1 Sentry
    • (-) Mini-Sentry health reduced to 80
