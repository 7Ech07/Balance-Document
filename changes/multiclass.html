Hitscan Weapons

Most hitscan weapons have a 1.25 second cooldown after firing before the weapon becomes perfectly accurate again (Shotguns and Miniguns have a 0.25 second cooldown instead). For some reason, the accuracy stays bad for the entire 1.25 seconds and suddenly becomes perfect again, instead of gradually becoming more accurate as you would intuitively expect.

This is mostly just relevant to the Revolvers (except the Ambassador, which already has this). The other weapons are getting it for the sake of consistency.

  • Changes:
    • (+) Accuracy on hitscan weapons steadily improves the longer you wait between shots, reaching perfect accuracy after 1.0 seconds.

Projectile Weapons

This is maybe a bit petty, but it’s always annoyed me how projectile weapons have a left/right bias that you need to compensate for up close. Making projectiles always fire from the centre would be ugly and make it harder to shoot around corners, so I came up with this as a compromise that is hopefully minimally obtrusive. I chose 200 HU since it’s the minimum distance at which projectile aim correction works.

  • Changes:
    • (~) When firing at a player within 200 HU, the projectile will be spawned out of the centre of the screen, regardless of weapon or viewmodel location.

Shotgun

For some bizarre reason, there are four (technically five) variants of the stock Shotgun in the game’s code, and some of them have slightly different stats. Soldier and Pyro have a slightly slower reload on their stock Shotguns than Heavy and Engineer. None of their reload speeds also align correctly with the reload speeds of any of the unlocks (according to the Wiki, at least). It’s minor, but there’s no reason for this to be a thing.

  • Changes:
    • (~) Reload speed for the first shell of all variants standardised at 1.0 seconds.

Pistol

This is much more commonly known, but Engineer’s Pistol also has different stats to Scout’s. This also has no reason to be a thing.

  • Changes:
    • (+) Reload speed standardised at 1.005 seconds (buff to Engineer, no change to Scout)
    • (~) Reserve ammo standardised at 48 (big nerf to Engineer, Scout gets an extra mag, and a few more bullets from ammo sources)

Reserve Shooter

This item is capable of shutting down blast jumping because the Mini-Crits don’t care whose explosion was responsible for launching the target airborne. Additionally, it is somewhat outclassed by the Panic Attack as the ‘combo Shotgun’ (especially on Pyro).

To justify this weapon existing on Pyro, I gave it the ability to combo with the primaries like on Soldier. Pyro would probably also appreciate a longer-ranged secondary, and it would still fill the niche of a fast-swapping weapon without the Panic Attack’s downsides.

  • Goal: Prevent Mini-Crits on blast jumpers, and add another attribute to expand on the item’s identity
  • Changes:
    • (+) Added: +20% more accurate
    • (-) Added: 20% damage penalty
    • (-) Only Mini-Crits targets launched by the user’s explosives
      • This applies to reflected projectiles and the projectiles from the Flame Burst attack, Phlogistinator and Dragon’s Fury (see Pyro section), but not Airblast

B.A.S.E. Jumper

This item isn’t very useful, especially after its nerf. While I do think it deserved to be looked at , I think the nerf went a bit too far.

  • Goal: Partially revert the nerf to its aerial mobility
  • Changes:
    • (+) Added: Parachute can be redeployed multiple times with a 0.5 second cooldown
    • (-) Increase air control with the parachute deployed by 100% (reversion)
    • (-) Added: While on fire, the user can hover in mid-air
      • The original version of the item used to do this; It’s funny, without being super problematic

Melee Combat

Users of melee complain that swings are often eaten by teammates since the game prioritises them over enemies for some reason. While we're here, I'd also like to add something to spice up melee a bit in a world without Random Crits.

  • Changes:
    • (+) Added: Third successful hit in a row Crits
    • The degree of forgiveness is higher than what the Gunslinger currently has (the Gunslinger has a timer of 1 second between swings, so perhaps 1.5 seconds instead)
    • (+) Melee attacks now penetrate players, damaging all enemies in the path of the swing even if a teammate is in the way
    • Backstabs will still hit through teammates, but will only hit the enemy closest to the Spy

Pain Train

The idea of a weapon that interacts specifically with objectives is interesting, but the Pain Train's big problem is that it only works in four game modes and only when attacking. I gave the weapon an upside that applies in every game mode, even those without conventional objectives.

I also decided to give it to Pyro. The weapon was originally intended for Pyro and I have no idea why they didn't go through with it. I think it would be cool on Pyro and would help them be better at their originally-intended offensive role.

  • Changes:
    • (+) Added: Now equippable on Pyro
    • (+) Added: Gain a speed boost when carrying an Intelligence briefcase
      • The Robot Destruction reactor core also counts as a briefcase
      • Hidden: standing near the player carrying the Pass Time JACK also grants the speed boost
    • (+) Added: Increased objective pickup radius on wearer
      • You have the Scout's money magnet (from MvM) but it applies to Player and Robot Destruction pickups as well as money
      • You do not have the Scout's heal-on-pickup
    • (+) Added: Gain 20 health per second while standing on the other team’s objective (their Payload cart, or a point you own)
      • This is the equivalent of a Level 3 Dispenser (like you get from your own team’s Payload carts, but better)
    • (+) Removed: 10% bullet damage vulnerability
    • (-) Added: -15 max health on wearer
